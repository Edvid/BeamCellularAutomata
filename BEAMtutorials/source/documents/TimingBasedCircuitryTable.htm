XOR pulse Gets activated When input signal switches
AND pulse (x+y) Gets activated only when input goes from LOW to HIGH. This is achieved by having inverted x, and delaying the breaking of x, so that y reaches the gate, before x is broken. The longer the delay, the longer the pulse of this AND pulse will be.
Delay Delays a signal
Clock A beamer with a delay attached to it, that periodically breaks the signal, creating a clock that sends out LOW and HIGH at equal lengths
Flip flop An SR NOR latch flip flop, with output Q and its complimentary Q. S for SET, R for RESET. When S recieves HIGH, then the flip flop's Q is set to HIGH. When R recieves HIGH, the flip flop's Q is reset to LOW. Q always act opposite of Q. ***
Pulsed Switch A flip flop, with two AND gates attached to it's input. Both sharing a former input, and each having either Q or Q, as to either SET or RESET depending on the state of the flip flop, from just a single input. An AND pulse (with inverted x) is added before this input, with a long delay on x.
byte counter: A series of 8 Pulsed Switches in a row, with a clock at the beginning, making for an 8 bit counter that counts up once every clock cycle.
